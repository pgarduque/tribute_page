Jim Thorpe,Olympic Champion

Jim Thorpe, in uniform at the 1912 Olympics in Stockholm,Sweden.
  • 1887 - Jim Thorpe born near Prague,Oklahoma. He also was possibly born near Indian Territory.
  • 1907 - Thorpe begins his athletic career under Glen Scobey"Pop" Warner.He competes in not only track and field,but football, baseball,lacrosse, and ballroom dancing.
  • 1909 - Played professional baseball for the Eastern Carolina League in North Carolina until 1910.This would later cost him his Olympic medals.
  • 1912 - Competes in the Olympics in Stockholm, Sweden. He performs in the first official pentathlon and decathlon,winning the gold for each.
  • 1913 - Thanks to an article written by the Worcester Telegram and other newspapers,the American Athletic Union revoked his amateur status and the IOC stripped him of his gold medals.He and his descendants would spend years appealing this decision.Thanks to the controversy, he signs as a free agent player with both the New York Giants and a football league,Pine Village Pros.He would play professional baseball until 1922.
  • 1915 - Signed with the Canton Bulldogs, one of the founding teams of the American Professional Football Association, which would later become the NFL.
  • 1921 - Helped to organize and played for the Ooorang Indians,an all-Native American football team.
  • 1926 - Played basketball for two years with "Jim Thorpe and his World-Famous Indians".This was not discovered until 2005.
  • 1928 - Retired from professional football at the age of 41. He spent the depression working multiple jobs and battling alcoholism.
  • 1953 - Jim thorpe dies in poverty from heart failure.
  • 1982 - Thanks to the Jim Thorpe Foundation,the IOC reinstated Jim Thorpe's amateur status and declared him a co-champion with the silver and bronze medalists, who had always stated that he was the only champion.
  • 1983 - In a special ceremony, the IOC presented copies of his medals to his family,the originals had been stolen many years previously and never recovered.
